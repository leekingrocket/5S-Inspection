About This App
The 5S Inspector 5S Inspector uses AI to analyse workspace photographs for compliance with the 5S Kaizen methodology — Sort, Set in Order, Shine, Standardise, and Sustain. It generates prioritised improvement tasks, stores annotated images for later review, and tracks completion across departments.

For technical issues, login problems, or feature requests, contact the support team using the details above.
5S Kaizen — Lean Workplace Methodology
Originally developed as part of the Toyota Production System, 5S is a systematic approach to workplace organisation. Each S builds on the last — together they create a safer, more efficient, and more consistent working environment. Tap any section below to expand.
1S
Sort — Seiri
Remove everything that isn't needed
▼
Eliminate clutter by removing everything from the workspace that is not required for current operations.
Go through every item in the area. If an item is not needed, not used regularly, or does not belong — remove it. Use red tags to flag items for review. This step alone can free significant floor space, reduce search time, and eliminate safety hazards caused by unnecessary items in walkways or on benches.
Key Questions
Is this item needed for current work? If not, why is it here?
How often is this used? Items used less than once a month should be questioned.
Is there more of this than we actually need right now?
Could this be a hazard, obstacle, or source of confusion for other workers?
2S
Set in Order — Seiton
A place for everything, everything in its place
▼
Arrange and label every remaining item so anyone can find it, use it, and return it in under 30 seconds.
Define a specific, logical home for every tool, component, document, and piece of equipment. Use shadow boards, floor markings, labels, and colour coding to make the correct location immediately obvious. The goal is that anyone — including a new starter — can find what they need and know where to return it without asking.
Key Actions
Create shadow boards for hand tools — outlines show what belongs where.
Use floor tape to mark equipment zones, walkways, and storage areas.
Label shelves, drawers, and bins with contents and maximum quantities.
Position frequently used items closest to the point of use.
3S
Shine — Seisō
Clean as a form of inspection
▼
Clean the workspace thoroughly — and use cleaning as an opportunity to inspect for defects, leaks, and damage.
Shine is not just about aesthetics. When you clean a machine or surface, you see it up close. This reveals oil leaks, loose bolts, worn components, and early signs of failure that would otherwise go unnoticed. Cleaning is inspection — and it prevents defects before they become problems. Assign ownership so every area has a responsible person.
Key Actions
Clean machines, benches, and floors as part of end-of-shift routine.
While cleaning, look for leaks, cracks, unusual wear, or loose fittings.
Address root causes — if oil leaks, fix the source, not just the puddle.
Assign named ownership for each zone with a visible cleaning schedule.
4S
Standardise — Seiketsu
Make the right way the only way
▼
Create visual standards and procedures so that Sort, Set in Order, and Shine are maintained consistently by everyone.
Standardise means documenting the best practice and making it visible. Use photos of correct workstation layouts, colour-coded zone maps, cleaning checklists, and posted schedules. The standard should be so clear that anyone can see at a glance whether the area is correct — no specialist knowledge required. This is how 5S is sustained across shift changes and staff turnover.
Key Actions
Post "5S Standard" photos showing the correct state of each workstation.
Create and display cleaning and audit schedules with named owners.
Use consistent colour coding across all departments (e.g. yellow = caution zones).
Integrate 5S checks into existing handover or shift-start routines.
5S
Sustain — Shitsuke
Build the discipline to maintain it
▼
Make 5S a habit through regular audits, visible results, recognition, and continuous improvement — not a one-off event.
The hardest S to achieve — because it relies on culture, not just process. Sustain means conducting regular 5S audits, sharing results with the team, recognising improvement, and treating backsliding as a problem to solve rather than a personal failure. This app is itself a Sustain tool — it makes scanning and logging issues quick enough that it actually gets done.
Key Actions
Conduct regular 5S audits using this app — log findings immediately.
Post scores and trends on the team audit board where everyone can see.
Celebrate improvement — recognise teams that raise their scores.
Treat declining scores as a process problem to investigate, not just a behaviour problem.
